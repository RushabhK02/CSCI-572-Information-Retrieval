Cold Spring Harbor Whaling Museum

Various pieces of scrimshaw, inked carvings on bone, are on display at the Cold Spring Harbor Whaling Museum in Cold Spring Harbor. (2010) Photo Credit: Handout

February 4, 2011 9:43 AM
Print
Share Tweet Share Email

Visitors can trace the history of whaling in Cold Spring Harbor and see real whale bones at the Cold Spring Harbor Whaling Museum.

This photo of crewmen manning a whaling boat and others pertaining to whaling are on display at the Cold Spring Harbor Whaling Museum in Cold Spring Harbor. (2010)

Children ages 6 to 10 can attend a pajama party for Valentine's Day, while Mom and Dad celebrate the holiday as a couple. Bring pajamas and a pillow for an evening of pizza, games, arts and crafts and a movie, 5 to 8 p.m. at the Cold Spring Harbor Whaling Museum, 301 Main St. (Route 25A), Cold Spring Harbor. Advance reservations required. $25 per child, 15 percent sibling discount. 631-367-3418; cshwhalingmuseum.org.
